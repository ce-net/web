## 1. What the commercial licence does

In exchange for the agreed fee and terms, Leif grants the licensee a non-exclusive,
non-transferable, non-sublicensable licence to use the specified ce-net repositories **without**
the AGPL-3.0 copyleft and network-source-disclosure obligations — i.e. the licensee may use
ce-net in closed-source, proprietary, and for-profit products and services, within the scope
defined in the agreement.

## 2. Scope to be negotiated per deal

Each commercial agreement fixes at least:

- **Repositories / components** covered (e.g. `ce`, `ce-rs`, named `ce-*` repos, or the whole project).
- **Permitted use** (internal use; embedding in a product; SaaS operation; AI training/fine-tuning; etc.).
- **Field of use and any AI restrictions.** Closed or proprietary AI training, fine-tuning,
  distillation, or model-grounding on ce-net is licensed **only if expressly granted** in the
  agreement, and may be excluded, conditioned, or separately priced at Leif's discretion.
- **Distribution rights** (whether the licensee may ship ce-net to its own customers, and on
  what terms those customers are bound — they remain unable to relicense and must come through
  Leif for any use outside the licensee's product).
- **Term, territory, exclusivity** (default: non-exclusive, worldwide, perpetual-for-the-version
  or subscription, as agreed).
- **Fee** (one-time, per-seat, per-deployment, revenue-share, or subscription — set per deal).
- **Support / updates / warranty / indemnity** (default: provided AS-IS with no warranty unless
  separately agreed and priced).
- **Audit and compliance** rights.

- No commercial rights arise until the agreement is signed by Leif.

- Breach of the commercial terms reverts the licensee to AGPL-3.0 only — under which their
